## Overview
ISMS Programme & ISO 27001 Certification Readiness designs, implements, and matures an Information Security Management System (ISMS) aligned to ISO/IEC 27001:2022, culminating in certification readiness. The service covers scope definition, risk treatment planning, Annex A control implementation, and internal audit preparation, giving mid-market organisations a defensible, auditable security management framework that satisfies customer, regulatory, and cyber-insurance requirements simultaneously.

## What's Included
- ISMS scope definition: organisational boundary, asset and service scope, exclusion justifications
- Information security risk assessment: threat and vulnerability identification, impact and likelihood rating, risk owner assignment (aligned to ISO 27005)
- Statement of Applicability (SoA): full Annex A control mapping with applicability decisions and implementation evidence
- Control implementation guidance across all applicable Annex A domains (organisational, people, physical, technological controls)
- Policy and procedure documentation: core policy set (ISSP, acceptable use, access control, incident management, supplier security)
- Internal audit programme: audit schedule, checklists, non-conformance tracking, and management review facilitation
- Stage 1 and Stage 2 audit preparation: evidence pack assembly and pre-audit gap walkthrough
- Ongoing ISMS maintenance: annual review cycle, continual improvement tracking, and management system updates

## What's Not Included
- ISO 27001 certification body fees and the certification audit itself (conducted by an accredited certification body)
- Penetration testing and red team exercises (available as a separate engagement)
- Legal or regulatory compliance advisory beyond information security controls (see [[NIS2_Entity_Classification]], [[GDPR_Gap_Assessment]])
- Enterprise risk management covering non-information-security business risks
